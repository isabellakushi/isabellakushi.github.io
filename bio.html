BIO

Teruko Isabella Kushi is a Japanese-American artist from Great Barrington, Massachusetts. Raised in a macrobiotic and waldorf community, Teruko received an alternative education in art and sustainable agriculture. Choosing to leave high school early to pursue a career in the arts, Teruko began working as a studio assistant; where she received a rigorous and classical training in oil painting and sculpture. Since being a student at the SMFA she has concentrated her degree in postmodern literature, sculpture, painting and digital media arts. She is the recipient of the prestigious Dana Pond Award in painting, the Linde Family Scholarship, and an honorable mention recipient of the Boit Award. She recently completed the competitive Senior Thesis program, and after graduation will continue her career assisting an installation artist in Japan.
